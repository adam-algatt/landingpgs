2023 Hyundai Ioniq 5 V.S. Mustang Mach E

Mach E

The 2023 Mustang Mach-E offers a potent mix in the quickly expanding EV industry. The all-electric SUV won praise for combining thrilling performance, eye-catching appearance, cutting-edge technology, a joyful driving experience, and something the rival brands can't match: the heritage of the Mustang name. Midway through 2023, the Mustang Mach-E switched to a new standard-range battery with 72 kWh (instead of the earlier model's 70 kWh). Let's examine how the Mustang MachE fares in comparison to its main all-electric SUV rivals. The IIHS Top Safety Pick award for 2022 goes to the Mach-E. Ford BlueCruise 1.3 is now included as standard.

2023 Hyundai Ioniq 5

The Build & Price website for the all-electric Ioniq 5 states that it is only offered in a few units. Despite its restricted availability, Ioniq 5 has received favorable reviews. On a single charge, the Ioniq 5's two battery options have ranges of up to 220 miles (standard range, RWD) or 303/266 miles (long range, RWD/AWD). However, we discovered a few justifications why the Mustang Mach-E is a better option

Comprehensive Comparison

Power

In terms of conventional power ratings, the Mustang Mach-E outperforms the Ioniq 5 by delivering more horsepower and torque in RWD variants and more horsepower in eAWD vehicles. Worth Noting: Buyers of the Ioniq 5 will have a lesser horsepower rating than a comparable Mustang Mach-E model with a maximum range of 303 miles (RWD, long-range). With standard eAWD, the California Route 1 variant has a range of 312 miles(1) and 346 horsepower, according to the EPA.2) Nothing Similar to the GT The GT series and GT Performance Edition of the Mustang Mach-E are both available, and both include a stronger secondary e-motor for maximum performance. There isn't a model from Ioniq 5 that can be compared to the GT.

Noteable: The Mustang Mach-E GT Performance Edition can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in the region of 3.5 seconds, which is another benefit. The AWD version of the Ioniq 5 can reach 60 mph in 5.2 seconds.

Range Comparison

Mustang Mach-E also has important range advantages. Customers who select the Select trim level, which comes with RWD and a standard-range battery, will have a range of 250 miles, according to the EPA. The range of the entry-level Ioniq 5 SE is estimated by the EPA to be 220 miles. The longest ranges supplied by each manufacturer also highlight an intriguing distinction: the Mustang Mach-E California Route 1 model has an EPA-estimated maximum range of 312 miles while simultaneously providing the advantages of standard all-wheel drive. The Ioniq 5 comes with special features like Vehicle-to-Load power, which includes exportable power and vehicle-to-vehicle charging, as well as the Blind Spot View Monitor, which shows photos of what is in the blind spot. These Ioniq 5 features are not featured in the lower trim levels and are only accessible on the model with the highest trim level (Limited). Ioniq 5 has the capacity to charge really quickly. Owners may recharge the battery from 10% to 80% in 18 minutes thanks to the battery's 350-kW, 800-volt capacity. The Mustang Mach-E does not currently have this feature. However, the Mach-E had many additional advantages that will help you to choose the Mach-E. Members of the BlueOval Charge Network who purchase a Mustang Mach-E will receive 250 kWh of free DC fast charging at Electrify America stations as well as access to more than 84,000 plugs at various charging stations across the nation.
